# Time to Contact vs Bat Speed / Power — Methodology Note

Status: **doctrine for future Phase 2 metric work.** No code changes. No formula changes. Captured verbatim from user direction.

## Core principle

**Time to Contact and Bat Speed are NOT interchangeable concepts.** Any future metric discussion must treat them as separate baseball qualities. They are not substitutes for one another unless evidence supports a different approach.

## Time to Contact

Measures:
- **Quickness** of the swing
- **Efficiency** of the kinetic chain
- Gives hitters **more time to see the baseball** before having to commit
- Improves **adjustability** mid-swing
- Is **one of the strongest separators of high-level hitters** from the rest

This is a *commitment-window* quality. It is what allows an elite hitter to wait longer, see more, and still get to the ball.

## Bat Speed

Measures:
- **Power production**
- **Damage potential** on contact
- Remains a highly valuable tool

This is a *contact-output* quality. It is what determines how hard the ball leaves the bat once contact is made.

## The elite combination

An elite hitter who combines **exceptional Time to Contact with exceptional Bat Speed** dramatically increases their ceiling and impact potential. The two compound: faster time to contact buys more decision time, and high bat speed monetizes the better decisions that result. Either one alone is incomplete.

## Rules for future metric work

1. Time to Contact must never be presented as a substitute for Bat Speed, or vice versa.
2. A redesign that replaces "Bat Speed" with "Hand Speed at Launch" (currently under discussion in feedback triage) does **not** replace Time to Contact. They are distinct quality vectors and must be evaluated independently on the report card.
3. "Hands-to-contact frame count" (proposed earlier) is a Time to Contact measurement, not a power measurement.
4. Any future composite score that blends them must expose both component scores separately on the report card and must justify the blending with evidence.
5. Any deviation from this doctrine requires explicit user authorization with evidence.

## Implementation gate

No metric changes in this area until:
1. Determinism investigation completes.
2. User authorizes the specific replacement.
3. Reference back to this memo is cited in the implementation plan.
