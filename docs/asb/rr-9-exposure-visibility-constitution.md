# RR-9 — Exposure & Visibility Ethics Constitution

**Status:** Sealed (Post-Mastery doctrine phase). Doctrine only.
**Scope:** the `relational.exposure.*` topic family and any future
discoverability, showcase, or recruiting-exposure surfaces derived
from it.
**Subordinate to:** Eternal Laws, Megaphase 151–160, RR-1…RR-8, the
safeguarding orchestration sub-route, minor-athlete supremacy, the
relational visibility matrix, and all prior immutable invariants
across Phases 1–150.

Visibility is earned through explicit pathways, never engineered
through engagement loops or popularity mechanics.

## RR-9 invariants

1. **Exposure never overrides safeguarding.** Safeguarding
   classifications preempt any exposure surface, recommendation,
   ranking, or notification fan-out.
2. **Minors remain protection-first.** For minor athletes, default
   exposure scope is closed. Any external exposure requires an active
   parent relationship and an explicit, revocable, lineage-visible
   authorization event.
3. **Visibility is earned through explicit pathways.** Discoverability
   surfaces require canonical opt-in events (athlete plus parent for
   minors). Implicit, inferred, or "smart default" exposure is
   forbidden.
4. **No algorithmic popularity loops.** Surfaces may not amplify based
   on view counts, follower counts, engagement velocity, share
   velocity, or social momentum. Ranking that rewards engagement is
   forbidden.
5. **No exploitative ranking systems.** Composite "exposure score" or
   "visibility rank" surfaces are forbidden unless every contributing
   antecedent is lineage-decomposable and the surface exposes the
   ranking method to the athlete.
6. **Athlete dignity preserved under visibility.** Exposure surfaces
   may not strip context, decontextualize highlights to fabricate
   narrative (RR-5 supremacy), or expose RR-8 life context.
7. **Exposure systems remain replay-visible.** Every exposure event
   (observed / aggregated / ingested_external) reconstructs
   deterministically at pinned `engine_version` + `reasoning_version`.
8. **Revocation is immediate.** Revoking exposure authorization
   removes downstream visibility on the next projection rebuild.
   External caches outside the canonical ledger are not authoritative;
   the ledger is.
9. **Commercial pressure may not relax exposure gates.** Recruiter
   demand, showcase economics, monetization incentive, and platform
   growth pressure may never widen the exposure aperture beyond
   athlete/parent authorization.
10. **Exposure never authors organism truth.** Exposure events never
    set `organism_truth`, `athlete_intent`, `authority_override`,
    `hard_stop`, or `rehabilitation_state`. They contribute lineage to
    bounded visibility decisions only.

## Allowable exposure pathways

- Athlete-authored profile publication with explicit scope
  (and parent co-authorization for minors).
- Coach-shared highlights with athlete approval per event.
- Recruiter access bound by RR-10 contact gates and RR-4 active
  relationship checks.
- Federation/league integrations via replay-safe adapter governance
  per Megaphase 111–150 federation_event doctrine.

## Prohibited engagement optimization

- "People who viewed your profile" loops.
- View-count or share-count ranking.
- Notification cadence tuned to engagement, not to event criticality.
- Hidden ranking that surfaces some athletes over others based on
  commercial signals.
- Pay-to-rank, pay-to-feature, pay-to-surface mechanics.
- "Trending athlete" or popularity feeds.

## Safeguarding precedence over visibility

- Active safeguarding flag → exposure surfaces redact, suppress, or
  pause without notice to non-safeguarding observers.
- Minor without active parent relationship → exposure aperture closed
  by default.
- Revoked parent relationship → previously authorized exposures lose
  downstream visibility immediately.
