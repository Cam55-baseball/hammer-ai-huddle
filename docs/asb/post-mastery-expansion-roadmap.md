# Post-Mastery Expansion Roadmap

**Status:** Doctrine-only ordering. Implementation deferred.
**Subordinate to:** Eternal Laws, Megaphase 151–160, RR-1…RR-10, all
prior immutable invariants across Phases 1–150.

This is the strict order of activation for the relational organism.
No step may begin until its activation prerequisites are met. No step
may be reordered, parallelized across categories, or merged with
another to satisfy product or commercial pressure.

---

## 1. Narrative system activation (RR-5)

- **Objective:** Replay-derived memory resurfacing, slump continuity,
  emotional-replay surfaces, and athlete-owned retrospectives.
- **Dependencies:** RR-4 sealed (✅). Conversation + psychological_state
  primitives operational (✅ Phase 152–154). Safeguarding orchestration
  sub-route operational (✅ Phase D).
- **Safeguarding constraints:** RR-5 invariant 6; narrative surfaces
  defer to safeguarding sub-route; minor visibility narrows by default.
- **Replay constraints:** Byte-identical narrative output under
  identical ledger inputs at pinned engine_version + reasoning_version.
  Revocation effective on next rebuild.
- **Operational risk:** Memory resurfacing surfaces increase ledger
  read pressure; projection caching must remain lineage-safe.
- **Emotional risk:** Highest. Narrative is the surface most prone to
  manipulation and identity capture. RR-5 invariants 3–5, 9 must be
  enforced in code, not just doctrine.
- **Activation prerequisites:** RR-5 doctrine sealed (✅). Narrative
  emitter + replay projection + lineage-visible callback UI primitive
  designed. Manipulation-pattern review committed.

## 2. Injury continuity activation (RR-6)

- **Objective:** Lifecycle events (onset, assessed, diagnosed,
  rtp_planned, milestone, setback, cleared, recurrence_watch),
  observational rehab surfaces, RTP continuity.
- **Dependencies:** Step 1 narrative does not gate step 2. RR-4 sealed
  (✅). Clinician relationship role available via RR-4.
- **Safeguarding constraints:** RR-6 invariants 4, 9. Parent supremacy
  for minors on every RR-6 surface.
- **Replay constraints:** RTP transitions deterministically
  reconstruct from ledger; clearance events are additive only.
- **Operational risk:** False auto-clearance is the highest-risk
  failure mode; missingness handling must be visible and conservative.
- **Emotional risk:** Medium-high. Pain self-reports must never be
  contradicted by inferred readiness.
- **Activation prerequisites:** RR-6 doctrine sealed (✅). Clinician
  role taxonomy + delegation model designed. RTP authorization
  workflow with explicit human action.

## 3. Life-context integration (RR-8)

- **Objective:** Disclosure-controlled academic / social / family /
  non-sport context informing bounded recommendations.
- **Dependencies:** Steps 1–2 do not gate step 3. RR-4 sealed (✅).
- **Safeguarding constraints:** RR-8 invariant 8; default scope self
  or parent for minors.
- **Replay constraints:** Disclosures revocable; visibility narrows on
  next rebuild.
- **Operational risk:** Surveillance drift. Any background ingestion
  surface must be reviewed against RR-8 invariant 1 before landing.
- **Emotional risk:** High. Coercive disclosure framing is forbidden.
- **Activation prerequisites:** RR-8 doctrine sealed (✅). Disclosure
  UI patterns reviewed for non-coercion. Safeguarding routing wired.

## 4. Exposure system activation (RR-9)

- **Objective:** Opt-in discoverability and showcase pathways with
  athlete (and parent for minors) authorization.
- **Dependencies:** Step 3 should land first to ensure life-context
  redaction in exposure surfaces. RR-4 sealed (✅).
- **Safeguarding constraints:** RR-9 invariants 1–2; minor exposure
  closed by default.
- **Replay constraints:** Every exposure event replay-reconstructable;
  revocation immediate.
- **Operational risk:** Highest. Engagement-loop reintroduction risk
  must be reviewed every release against RR-9 invariant 4.
- **Emotional risk:** High. Dignity and ranking-system risk concentrate
  here.
- **Activation prerequisites:** RR-9 doctrine sealed (✅). Exposure
  authorization workflow designed. Anti-popularity-loop review
  committed.

## 5. Recruiter workflow activation (RR-10)

- **Objective:** Recruiter inquiries, responses, meetings, offers,
  decisions, withdrawals, gate violations.
- **Dependencies:** Steps 3 and 4 must land first. RR-4 sealed (✅).
  Developmental gating matrix (✅ Phase 154).
- **Safeguarding constraints:** RR-10 invariants 1, 4, 5. Parent
  supremacy non-optional for minors. Cadence limits per developmental
  matrix.
- **Replay constraints:** All recruiter events replay-reconstructable;
  gate violations are first-class canonical events.
- **Operational risk:** Highest. Pay-to-win and ranking-manipulation
  pressure concentrate here.
- **Emotional risk:** Highest. Pressure-escalation on minors must be
  forbidden in code.
- **Activation prerequisites:** RR-10 doctrine sealed (✅). Recruiter
  role taxonomy. Gate-violation event topic registered. Cadence-limit
  enforcer designed.

## 6. Career arc activation (RR-7)

- **Objective:** Replay-derived longitudinal summaries with athlete-
  owned redirection.
- **Dependencies:** Steps 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 should land first so the arc
  composes from real ledger surfaces, not placeholder data.
- **Safeguarding constraints:** RR-7 invariant 9; recruiter-visible
  arcs require parent authorization for minors.
- **Replay constraints:** Arc snapshots replay-reconstructable; prior
  snapshots remain available, not promoted to current authority.
- **Operational risk:** Medium. Cache-as-authority is the primary
  failure shape.
- **Emotional risk:** High. Destiny framing and identity locking risk
  concentrate here.
- **Activation prerequisites:** RR-7 doctrine sealed (✅). Athlete
  goal-statement event UI. Anti-destiny-framing copy review.

## 7. Observability + orchestration

- **Objective:** Cross-RR observability dashboards, gate-violation
  audit surfaces, safeguarding-route audit, replay-equivalence
  verification, lineage decomposition tools.
- **Dependencies:** Steps 1–6 operational.
- **Safeguarding constraints:** Observability surfaces inherit RR-8
  and RR-9 scope; never leak life context or exposure beyond
  authorization.
- **Replay constraints:** Observability surfaces are replay-
  reconstructable; cost-driven decimation forbidden (per Megaphase 76–
  90 observability supremacy).
- **Operational risk:** Medium. Observability schema must remain
  frozen per the observability schema lock (Megaphase 63–67).
- **Emotional risk:** Low.
- **Activation prerequisites:** Steps 1–6 stable in production.

## 8. Scale hardening

- **Objective:** Distributed survivability, regional partitioning
  legality, fabric determinism at scale, persistence-authority
  validation, AI orchestration audit at scale.
- **Dependencies:** Step 7 operational.
- **Safeguarding constraints:** Scale operations may never bypass
  safeguarding routes under latency / cost / vendor framing.
- **Replay constraints:** Scaling preserves byte-identical replay
  across nodes; no region-local truth.
- **Operational risk:** Medium-high. Vendor-capture and region-local
  authority drift are the named failure shapes.
- **Emotional risk:** Low (indirect — only through scale-driven
  degradation).
- **Activation prerequisites:** Step 7 stable. Megaphase 76–90
  distributed survivability invariants enforced in deployment
  pipeline.
